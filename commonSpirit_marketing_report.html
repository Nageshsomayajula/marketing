CommonSpirit Marketing Performance Report

Data from 2025-03-01 to 2025-03-31

Executive Summary

Overall, marketing efforts resulted in 427.9 million impressions and 1.38 million clicks, leading to 14,740 conversions at a CPA of $210.64. While the total ad spend was $3.1 million, there are significant performance disparities across channels and markets. Paid Search and Display channels demonstrated strong conversion volume with reasonable CPAs, whereas Paid Social exhibited a high CPA with low conversions. Certain markets like Puget Sound and Sacramento showed efficient CPA, while others such as Houston and Utah had significantly higher CPAs. A strategic reallocation of budget and channel optimization, especially within Paid Social, is essential to improve overall marketing efficiency.

Strengths

  • Paid Search channel delivered 6,822 conversions with a CPA of $172.51, indicating strong performance and a good return on investment.
  • Display channel generated 4,245 conversions at a CPA of $166.94, demonstrating effectiveness in driving conversions at a reasonable cost.
  • Puget Sound market performed well with a CPA of $82.97 and 3,306 conversions, highlighting effective campaign targeting and messaging in this region.
  • Sacramento market achieved a CPA of $148.45 with 1,642 conversions, suggesting a successful strategy within that specific geography.
  • Advertising Cloud DSP showed relatively efficient performance with a CPA of $164.85 and 7,753 conversions, indicating a strong ad platform.
  • Connected TV, despite lower clicks, yielded a competitive CPA of $109.96 and 2,188 conversions, highlighting it as a potentially valuable channel.

Opportunities for Growth

  • Paid Social's CPA of $3,915.79 for only 165 conversions indicates significant inefficiency and warrants immediate investigation and potential budget reallocation.
  • Online Audio and YouTube OLV channels had zero conversions despite ad spend, suggesting they are underperforming and need reevaluation or discontinuation.
  • Houston and Utah markets have exceptionally high CPAs ($720.99 and $1,243.12, respectively), indicating a need for improved targeting or different channel strategies.
  • Several hospital/medical groups (e.g., CHI St Luke's Health Baylor St Luke's Medical Center Houston-TX (BSLMCH)) have high CPAs, suggesting performance inconsistencies across locations.
  • GoogleSearch and Other ad platforms have no data which could mean data tracking issues or campaigns need to be set up.
  • Some medical groups are not being tracked because their data is missing from the reports. This could be a data issue that needs addressing.

Recommendations

  • Reallocate budget from Paid Social to better-performing channels like Paid Search and Display, based on their lower CPAs and higher conversion rates.
  • Pause or significantly reduce ad spend on Online Audio and YouTube OLV channels, and explore alternative video marketing strategies with better ROI.
  • Conduct a thorough analysis of campaigns in Houston and Utah to identify the root causes of high CPAs, and implement targeted optimizations or alternative marketing approaches.
  • Implement A/B testing on ad creatives and targeting parameters within Paid Social to identify strategies that can improve conversion rates and lower CPA.
  • Investigate the performance discrepancies among hospital/medical groups and implement tailored strategies for underperforming locations, considering market-specific nuances.
  • Ensure all markets, especially those with zero data, are properly tracked and analyze why there are no conversions.
